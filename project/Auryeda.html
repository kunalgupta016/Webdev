Ayurveda - The Science of Life

Discover the ancient wisdom of Ayurveda, a natural system of medicine that originated in India more than 5,000 years ago. Below you'll find some of the most important Ayurvedic medicines and their benefits.

Ashwagandha

Known as 'Indian Winter Cherry' or 'Indian Ginseng', Ashwagandha is one of the most important herbs in Ayurveda.


Benefits:

  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Improves strength and muscle mass
  • Boosts immunity
  • Enhances memory and cognitive function

Usage:

Usually taken as a powder mixed with warm milk or water, typically 1-2 teaspoons daily.

Triphala

A traditional Ayurvedic formula combining three fruits: Amalaki, Bibhitaki, and Haritaki.


Benefits:

  • Supports digestive health
  • Natural gentle detoxifier
  • Improves eye health
  • Reduces inflammation

Usage:

Can be taken as tablets or powder, typically 1/2 to 1 teaspoon before bed with warm water.

Turmeric (Haldi)

A powerful anti-inflammatory herb that has been used in Ayurvedic medicine for thousands of years.


Benefits:

  • Reduces inflammation
  • Powerful antioxidant
  • Improves brain function
  • Supports joint health

Usage:

Can be added to food, taken as supplements, or mixed with milk as 'golden milk'.

Brahmi

Also known as Bacopa monnieri, Brahmi is a powerful herb known for its cognitive-enhancing properties.


Benefits:

  • Improves memory and concentration
  • Reduces anxiety and stress
  • Supports brain health
  • Enhances learning ability

Usage:

Available as tablets, powder, or liquid extract. Typically taken with warm water.

Amla (Indian Gooseberry)

One of the richest natural sources of Vitamin C and a powerful antioxidant.


Benefits:

  • Boosts immunity
  • Improves skin health
  • Enhances digestion
  • Supports heart health

Usage:

Can be consumed fresh, as juice, powder, or in supplement form.

Shatavari

Known as the 'Queen of Herbs' in Ayurveda, particularly beneficial for women's health.


Benefits:

  • Supports female reproductive health
  • Improves immune function
  • Enhances digestive health
  • Natural antioxidant

Usage:

Usually taken as powder with milk or water, or in tablet form.

Neem

A versatile herb known for its antibacterial and antifungal properties.


Benefits:

  • Purifies blood
  • Improves skin health
  • Supports immune system
  • Natural pesticide

Usage:

Available as tablets, powder, or oil. Can be used both internally and externally.

Guduchi

Also known as Giloy, it's considered one of the most versatile herbs in Ayurveda.


Benefits:

  • Boosts immunity
  • Reduces stress
  • Improves digestive health
  • Purifies blood

Usage:

Can be taken as juice, powder, or tablets.

Shankhpushpi

A brain tonic herb known for its cognitive enhancing properties.


Benefits:

  • Improves memory
  • Reduces anxiety
  • Enhances learning ability
  • Promotes sleep quality

Usage:

Usually taken as powder or syrup with milk or water.

Licorice (Mulethi)

A sweet root with numerous medicinal properties.


Benefits:

  • Soothes throat
  • Supports respiratory health
  • Aids digestion
  • Boosts immunity

Usage:

Can be consumed as tea, powder, or in tablet form.
